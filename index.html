What this is

The four things that make a gathering work.

Recurring
Same time, same place, every week

Repetition is what turns strangers into friends — it's the same reason you're still close to people from school or an old job.

Specific
The more specific, the better the people

Built around one activity, one community, one shared thing. The more specific your gathering, the better the people it naturally attracts.

Intentional
People who actually want to connect

Everyone on Gather has explicitly opted in to making real friends — that's the whole reason they're here. You start every gathering with a room that already wants what you're building.

Paid
A filter, not a barrier

People who pay show up. People who show up become regulars. Charging isn't just about income — it's a signal that this is worth taking seriously.

Getting started

Three steps to your first gathering.

1
Design your gathering

Pick something specific you genuinely care about — the more specific, the better. Then design a simple repeatable format that works for your activity. "Slow Sunday cook for sober queer people" is a gathering. "Dinner club" isn't. The hosting guide walks you through this in detail.

2
Run a trial, then go live

Your first gathering is a rehearsal — run it with friends, get photos, sharpen the format, collect your first reviews. Four people who enjoyed it is enough to go public. Create your gathering in the app, set your price, turn on recurring tickets, and we'll help you find the right people nearby.

3
Consistency is what turns a gathering into a community.

Encourage your first attendees to commit to five weeks — explain why it matters. By week three something shifts in the room. By week six, you've built something people would genuinely miss. That's when it stops being a gathering and starts being a community.
