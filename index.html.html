What this is

The four things that make a gathering work.

Recurring
Same time, same place, every week

Repetition is what turns strangers into friends — it's the same reason you're still close to people from school or an old job.

Specific
The more specific, the better the people

Built around one activity, one community, one shared thing. The specificity attracts the right people without you having to screen anyone yourself.

Intentional
You state the goal out loud

That everyone in the room will genuinely know each other by week six. That single declaration changes how people show up.

Paid
A filter, not a barrier

People who pay show up. People who show up become regulars. Charging isn't just about income — it's a signal that this is worth taking seriously.

Getting started

Five steps from idea to first gathering.

1
Pick your thing

Something specific you genuinely care about. The more specific, the better. "Dinner club" is too broad. "Slow Sunday cook for sober queer people" is a gathering.

2
Design your format

What happens when someone walks in the door? A simple, repeatable structure — opening check-in, shared activity, closing round — gives people something to hold onto and come back for. The hosting guide walks you through this.

3
Run a trial with friends first

Your first gathering is a rehearsal. Get photos, sharpen the format, collect your first reviews. It's normal for this to feel rough — that's what it's for. Four people who enjoyed it is enough to open publicly.

4
Go live on Gather

Create your gathering in the app, set your price, and turn on recurring tickets. We'll help you reach the right people — through the app and, if you want, through Instagram ads targeted to your city and community.

5
Show up. Then show up again.

Encourage your first attendees to commit to five weeks — explain why it matters. By week three something shifts in the room. By week six, you've built something people would genuinely miss. That's when it stops being a gathering and starts being a community.
